recount is a growing ecosystem of tools that makes archived genomics data easy to query and reanalyze.
recount3 [cite]: Access gene-, exon-, exon-exon junction, and base-resolution counts for >750,000 human and mouse RNA-seq samples.

Snaptron [cite]: Search for splicing events across >750,000 human and mouse RNA-seq samples.

snapcount [cite]: Use Snaptron in R.

ASCOT [cite]: Quantify and visualize alternative splicing across >70,000 human and mouse RNA-seq samples.

recountmethylation [cite]: Access noob-normalized Beta-values across >30,000 Illumina HumanMethylation450K array samples on GEO.
Developed at Johns Hopkins University, the Lieber Institute for Brain Development, and Oregon Health & Science University.
